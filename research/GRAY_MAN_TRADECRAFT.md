## Executive summary

The previous `GRAY_MAN_RESEARCH.md` was comprehensive but evidentially weak: most of its "psychology" and behavioral claims trace to survivalist/tradecraft blogs (rdctd.pro, trdcrft.com, tactical.com, cleverprepper.us, valortec.com) and are presented as settled fact. A credible rebuild re-anchors on three peer-reviewed/official pillars and ruthlessly grades each claim, retiring the unverifiable numbers and the offensive or quasi-illegal capabilities the old doc flirted with.

**Pillar 1 — Situational-awareness science is real, and it is Endsley's, not Cooper's.** Endsley's three-level model (perception → comprehension → projection) is the dominant, validated framework (Endsley, *Human Factors* 1995; Endsley 2015, "SA Misconceptions and Misunderstandings"). Aviation safety data attribute roughly 76% of SA failures to Level 1 (perception), meaning training should over-invest in noticing and scanning, not elaborate "projection." Cooper's Color Code is a useful pedagogical mnemonic for mindset escalation but is **not** a validated cognitive model and is widely misapplied as if awareness alone produces safety.

**Pillar 2 — Behavioral threat/deception detection from observable cues is the area to debunk.** This is the heart of "Left of Bang" folklore and of the old doc's "Anomaly Detector," "Behavioral Baseline Engine," and "Behavior Self-Check" features. The GAO found TSA's $1B+ SPOT program performed "the same as or slightly better than chance" against more than 400 studies over 60 years (GAO-14-159), and that 98% of the 178 sources TSA cited lacked valid evidence (GAO-17-608R). Bond & DePaulo's meta-analysis (206 studies, 24,483 judges) puts unaided human lie detection at 54%. The one defensible nuance: multi-cue *cluster* detection performs better (~68%) than single cues — a partial, contested rescue of the baseline-plus-anomaly idea, not a settled one.

**Pillar 3 — Digital signature reduction must be grounded in current, documented threats.** IMSI catchers downgrade phones to 2G to strip encryption (EFF; mitigated client-side by Android's Disable-2G toggle since Android 12 and iOS Lockdown Mode since iOS 17). MAC/BLE randomization is defeated by probe-request fingerprinting ("Bleach" 2024: up to 99% re-association). ALPR mass surveillance is now industrial scale (Flock: 20B+ scans/month, 5,000+ communities; EFF 2025 abuse investigations). BLE tracker stalking is empirically documented with a real cross-platform standard (Apple/Google DULT, IETF WG, finalized 2025) and proven OSS detection (AirGuard, Apache-2.0, on-device, TU Darmstadt SEEMOO).

The legal/ethical framing matters and the old doc ignored it: defensive counter-surveillance with a legitimate protective purpose is lawful; aiding another's evasion of lawful arrest, or surveilling-to-harass, is criminal (18 USC 2261A; obstruction). Greyline should ship calibrated, citation-backed training and detection tools — and explicitly cut the evasion-enabling content.

## Key findings

### Situational-awareness science: lead with Endsley, subordinate Cooper

**Endsley's three-level SA model is the genuine, validated science the product should lead with — not Cooper's colors.** Endsley 1995 (*Human Factors* 37(1):32–64) is among the most-cited human-factors papers, defining situational awareness as perception → comprehension → projection. Aviation safety data attribute roughly 76% of SA errors to Level 1 (perception), ~20% to Level 2, and ~3.4% to Level 3 — so training should over-invest in noticing and scanning, not in elaborate "projection" wizardry. Endsley 2015 ("Situation Awareness Misconceptions and Misunderstandings," https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/1555343415572631) rebuts the product-vs-process critique and is the citation that grounds the SA section. **Grade: Validated.**

**Cooper's Color Code is a mindset mnemonic, not a perception model.** The White/Yellow/Orange/Red ladder was, in Cooper's own framing, "about the degree of peril you are willing to do something about" — not a model of perception. Its popular reinterpretation as a "situational awareness" ladder is criticized (Breach Bang Clear) as "essentially useless and misleading," because awareness without comprehension does nothing. Greyline should present it as a simple mnemonic, explicitly subordinate to Endsley, and drop any claim that "Condition Yellow" is empirically protective. **Grade: Contested (as a mnemonic, useful; as science, no).**

### Behavioral threat / deception detection: the core thing to debunk

**Detecting threat or deception from observable single cues is not empirically supported — this is the single most important correction.** It is the core premise of "Left of Bang" and of the old doc's "Anomaly Detector," "Behavioral Baseline Engine," and "Behavior Self-Check" features. GAO-14-159 (2013, https://www.gao.gov/assets/gao-14-159.pdf) reviewed 4 meta-analyses / >400 studies / 60 years and found accuracy "the same as or slightly better than chance"; GAO-17-608R (2017, https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-17-608r) found 98% (175/178) of TSA's cited sources, and 77% being news/opinion, were not valid evidence. TSA's SPOT program cost ~$200M/yr (>$1B cumulative) and identified zero terrorists. **Grade: Folklore (as a reliable single-cue capability).**

**The defensible nuance worth keeping: clusters beat single cues.** Meta-analytic work on *clusters* of nonverbal behavior (not single tells) reports ~68% accuracy versus Bond & DePaulo's 54% single-judge baseline (206 studies, 24,483 judges; https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1207/s15327957pspr1003_2). So "baseline + anomaly" has a kernel of validity **if** framed as multi-cue pattern reading with explicit uncertainty. Greyline must not imply users can reliably spot "sinister intent," and should cite the contested status rather than the book jacket. **Grade: Contested.**

### Digital signature reduction: real, current threats

**IMSI catchers / cell-site simulators are a real, current threat with real client-side mitigations.** EFF documents that they force phones onto 2G to strip encryption and capture IMSI/metadata/content; they are used by FBI/DEA/ICE/military and local police (one sheriff used theirs 300+ times/year) (https://sls.eff.org/technologies/cell-site-simulators-imsi-catchers). Mitigations now exist client-side: Android Disable-2G toggle (since Android 12, AOSP), null-cipher protection (Android 14), IMSI/cipher notifications (Android 15/16, currently Pixel-only due to modem requirements) (https://source.android.com/docs/security/features/cellular-security/disable-2g), and iOS disables 2G in Lockdown Mode (iOS 17). Civilian **detection** apps remain unreliable — EFF notes SnoopSnitch "may not be verifiably accurate," and iOS forbids them. The honest posture: enable the OS mitigations; do not over-promise detection. **Grade: Validated (threat + mitigations); detection apps Contested.**

**MAC/BLE randomization is broken by probe-request fingerprinting.** The old doc implicitly trusted randomization ("WiFi/Bluetooth disable when not in use" while treating randomization as protective). Vanhoef et al. 2016 showed Information Elements plus sequence numbers defeat it; the "Bleach" system (2024) re-associates randomized MACs to physical devices with up to 99% accuracy using content and timing signatures (https://arxiv.org/html/2408.01578v1). Greyline's correct advice: turn radios **OFF** (do not rely on randomization), and treat Wi-Fi probe history as a tracking vector. **Grade: Validated.**

**ALPR is now mass surveillance at industrial scale and belongs in the threat model the old doc lacked.** Flock Safety operates in 5,000+ communities across 49 states and performs 20+ billion vehicle scans/month; crowdsourced DeFlock.me mapped 76,000+ readers. EFF's 2025 investigations (12M searches / 3,900 agencies, Dec 2024–Oct 2025) documented searches tied to protests, a self-managed-abortion dragnet, and slurs targeting Romani people (https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2025/12/effs-investigations-expose-flock-safetys-surveillance-abuses-2025-review). This is a far stronger "vary your routes / it is not paranoia" grounding than the old doc's blog cites. **Grade: Validated.**

### BLE tracker stalking: the biggest modernization gap

**BLE tracker stalking (AirTag etc.) is empirically documented and now standardized — a major modernization the 2024-era doc misses entirely.** CDC NISVS 2023/2024 finds 22.5% of women experience stalking, with technology-facilitated tactics affecting 16–29% of victims (https://www.cdc.gov/nisvs/media/pdfs/stalking-brief.pdf). Apple/Google's DULT spec (IETF DULT WG, charter approved, finalized 2025; cross-platform alerts shipped May 2024 in iOS 17.5 / Android 6+) is the standard (https://datatracker.ietf.org/group/dult/about/), and AirGuard (TU Darmstadt SEEMOO, Apache-2.0, 2300+ stars, on-device, last release Apr 2026) is the OSS reference (https://github.com/seemoo-lab/AirGuard). Academic evaluation (Northeastern 2023; USENIX Security 2025 CISPA, https://www.usenix.org/conference/usenixsecurity25/presentation/gerhardt) shows alerts are slow and unreliable, especially on Android — a real, teachable gap Greyline can fill. **Grade: Validated.**

### Legal and ethical boundaries the old doc ignored

**Defensive counter-surveillance is lawful; surveillance-to-harass and aiding evasion are crimes.** Detecting and documenting who follows *you* serves a "legitimate purpose" and is generally lawful. But 18 USC 2261A (https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2261A) makes surveillance-with-intent-to-harass — a "course of conduct" (2+ acts causing substantial emotional distress) — a federal crime, and knowingly helping another person evade lawful arrest is obstruction/accessory. The old doc's SDR Route Generator, dead-drop finder, "restraint escape," "social engineering to talk through checkpoints," and disguise/identity-swap content drift toward enabling unlawful evasion and must be reframed defensively or cut. **Grade: Validated (legal framing).**

### The codebase is already the correct template

**Greyline's real architecture already embodies the correct posture and should be the model for new work.** `lib/opsec.ts` explicitly cites EFF SSD, Freedom of the Press Foundation, CPJ, and GIJN (not blogs); `lib/self-doxxing.ts` performs zero network requests and only generates copy-ready queries; `lib/border.ts` is a deterministic exposure engine that states *why* and notes "nothing here is legal advice." New tradecraft features must match this evidence-graded, offline-first, non-enabling bar — the old research doc is the outlier, not the codebase.

**Practical citation upgrade.** Replace reliance on rdctd.pro/trdcrft.com/spyscape with GAO reports, Endsley's peer-reviewed papers, Bond & DePaulo (*Personality & Social Psychology Review* 2006), IETF DULT drafts, EFF Street-Level Surveillance, CDC NISVS, USENIX/CISPA tracker studies, AOSP cellular-security docs, and the Bleach/Vanhoef probe-request papers. Every retained gray-man claim should carry an evidence grade (Validated / Contested / Folklore).

## Legal & ethical guardrails

Greyline is a **defensive, lawful, anti-stalking** tool for privacy-conscious travelers, investigative journalists, and corporate travel-risk teams. It is not an evasion toolkit. The following guardrails are load-bearing for legal, reputational, and distribution (App Store / AGPL) reasons.

- **The statutory line (18 USC 2261A).** Defensive counter-surveillance — detecting and documenting who is following *you* — serves a "legitimate purpose" and is generally lawful. Surveillance carried out as a "course of conduct" (2+ acts) with intent to harass or that causes substantial emotional distress is a federal crime. Counter-surveillance in Greyline stays defensive (log + pattern detection of who follows the user) and never offensive (https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/2261A).
- **No aiding evasion of lawful authority.** Knowingly helping another person evade lawful arrest is obstruction/accessory conduct (18 USC Ch. 73). Features that could function as how-to evasion are out of scope.
- **Cut from Greyline (evasion-enabling).** The following old-doc concepts are removed, not reframed: **dead-drop finder**, **restraint / trunk-escape** content, **identity-swap / disguise** content, and **"social-engineer / talk through checkpoints."** The **SDR Route Generator** is cut as offensive route-evasion; counter-surveillance remains a defensive log-and-detect capability only.
- **Jurisdiction-aware context, not legal advice.** Any counter-surveillance, route, or identity-adjacent surface should attach jurisdiction context ("18 USC 2261A: surveillance-to-harass is a crime; this tool is for defending YOU") and reuse `border.ts`'s "state why / not legal advice" ethic.

- **ALPR/FRT coverage:** For the Threat-Model Wizard's ALPR/FRT advice, how much country-specific surveillance data can be bundled offline (DeFlock-style ALPR locations are US-centric and crowdsourced) without overpromising coverage outside North America?
